## Key Findings / Observations

### Trip Length (8.2.1)
- Strong monotonic increase: 13.9% speeding at 149m median distance to 54.2% at 2,168m
- Service roads (OR=2.74) and tertiary roads (OR=2.05) enable speeding; primary/secondary roads protective (OR<0.2)
- Riders consistently slow down in second half of trips (-1.5 km/h mean drift)

### Curvature (8.3.1-8.3.2)
- Counter-intuitive: higher curvature -> LOWER speeding (OR=0.716, p<0.001)
- Quintile range: 41% speeding (straightest) to 16.8% (curviest)
- Risk paradox: when speeding does occur on curvy roads, risk score is 5x higher
- Most e-scooter trips are curvy (49.9%) or mixed (45.0%); only 5.1% are straight

### SHAP (8.4.1)
- LightGBM achieves AUC-ROC=0.905 for speeding prediction
- TUB mode SHAP value (2.33) dominates all other features by 3.6x
- Top 5: TUB mode, log(distance), mean acceleration, cruise fraction, speed CV
- Infrastructure features (service, footway, residential fractions) appear in top 15
