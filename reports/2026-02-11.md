### Phase 2 Findings

#### Trip-Level Speed Indicators
- **Mean avg speed**: 14.86 km/h (from parsed speed profiles)
- **Mean max speed**: 22.95 km/h
- **Mean P85 speed**: 21.04 km/h
- **Mean speed CV**: 0.52 — substantial within-trip speed variability
- **Mean speeding rate** (fraction of points > 25 km/h): 4.88%
- **Mean speeding duration**: 15.0 seconds per trip
- **70.4% of trips have zero speeding** at any point
- Of trips that speed: 13.8% have 0-10% speeding rate, 9.2% have 10-25%, 5.5% have 25-50%

#### Acceleration/Deceleration (10s intervals)
- **Mean |acceleration|**: 0.126 m/s²
- **P95 max acceleration**: 0.67 m/s²
- **P95 max deceleration**: -0.67 m/s²
- **Harsh event threshold recalibrated**: 2 m/s² (config) is unreachable at 10s intervals; 0.5 m/s² used instead
- At 0.5 m/s² threshold: 25.4% of trips have harsh acceleration, 34.5% have harsh deceleration

#### Speed Profile Features
- **Mean cruise fraction**: 33.2% of trip time in "cruise" mode (within ±3 km/h of mean)
- **Mean zero-speed fraction**: 13.5% — significant idle/stopped time
- **Mean ramp-up duration**: 39.3s to reach 80% of max speed

#### User-Level Analysis (382,328 users)
- **Mean trips/user**: 7.3 (median: 3) — heavy-tailed distribution
- **32.3% one-time users**, 36.1% have 2-5 trips, 0.2% have 100+ trips
- **60.9% of users NEVER speed** (in observed trips)
- **17.3% are frequent speeders** (speeding in >50% of their trips)
- **9.6% are habitual speeders** (speeding in >75% of trips)
- **Mean speeding propensity**: 20.9% (but median is 0% — heavily right-skewed)
- **Weekend trip fraction**: 24.0%

### Speeding Concentration Finding (Paper Highlight)
The data reveals a clear **concentration of speeding behavior**: while 29.6% of all trips involve at least one speeding episode, this behavior is concentrated among a minority of users. 60.9% of users never exceed 25 km/h, while 17.3% of users speed on more than half their trips. This suggests that targeted interventions (e.g., speed governors for high-risk users) could be more effective than blanket speed limits.
