## Key Findings / Observations

### User-Level Placebo Test Results

| Metric | Placebo (Aug-Sep -> Oct) | Treatment (Oct-Nov -> Dec) |
|--------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| Switcher speed change | +0.274 km/h | -0.091 km/h |
| Never-TUB speed change | +0.357 km/h | -0.292 km/h |
| **DiD effect** | **-0.083 km/h** | **+0.201 km/h** |
| Cohen's d | -0.030 (negligible) | +0.069 (negligible) |
| Direction | Opposite to treatment | Treatment effect |
| Sample: switchers | 41,039 | 37,024 |
| Sample: never-TUB | 114,474 | 79,668 |

**Key insight**: The placebo DiD is in the OPPOSITE direction (-0.083 vs +0.201), meaning during the placebo period, never-TUB riders had a slightly larger speed increase than switchers. This rules out the possibility that switchers systematically diverge from never-TUB users over time, confirming the December effect is genuinely treatment-driven.

### Pre-existing Group Difference Stability

The pre-ban speed gap between switchers and never-TUB riders is remarkably stable across time windows:
- Placebo pre-period (Aug-Sep): +0.71 km/h (d = 0.255)
- Treatment pre-period (Oct-Nov): +0.65 km/h (d = 0.237)

This consistency further supports the composition interpretation: switchers are inherently faster riders regardless of time period.
