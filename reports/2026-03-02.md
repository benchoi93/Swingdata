## Key Findings / Observations

### December 2023 TUB Ban Discovery
- **Swing effectively banned TUB (turbo) mode in December 2023.** Market share collapsed from 54.6% to 1.4% in a single month. STD (standard) mode absorbed 85.8% of trips.
- **System-wide speeding rate collapsed from ~28% to ~1%.** This is the clearest evidence that speed governors causally reduce speeding.
- The ban was near-universal (50/54 cities), but pre-ban TUB adoption varied substantially (32-70%), providing treatment intensity variation for causal identification.

### DiD Model Results
| Model | Key Estimate | R² / Fit | Interpretation |
|-------|-------------|----------|---------------|
| 1. Cross-sectional OLS | β = -48.7 per unit TUB share | R² = 0.625 | 10pp higher pre-TUB -> 4.9pp larger speeding reduction |
| 2. TWFE DiD | β = -0.568 (p = 2.3e-14) | R² = 0.945 | City with 50% pre-TUB -> 28.4pp speeding reduction |
| 3. Event Study | Dec coef = -0.592 | — | 6x larger than any pre-period coefficient |
| 4. Dose-Response | β = 49.9 per unit TUB reduction | R² = 0.625 | 10pp TUB reduction -> 5.0pp speeding reduction |

### Robustness Checks (11.3.1-11.3.3)
| Check | Result | Verdict |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Restricted window (Apr-Dec) | Pre-trends still fail formally, but pre-trend is POSITIVE (+0.03 to +0.11). Dec coef = -0.591 | Conservative bias; treatment effect is underestimate |
| Placebo test (Sep 2023) | beta = -0.015, p = 0.561 | **PASS** — no effect without treatment |
| Heterogeneity (city size) | Q1: 25.4pp, Q2: 24.1pp, Q3: 22.9pp, Q4: 20.2pp (all p<0.001) | Effects in all quartiles |

### Caveats
- **Pre-trends fail formally** even in the restricted Apr-Dec window: May-Sep coefficients are significant at 5%. However, the pre-trend direction is POSITIVE (high-TUB cities had increasing speeding), meaning the estimated treatment effect is conservative — the true causal effect is likely larger.
- **Placebo test passes strongly**: Using Sep 2023 as fake treatment yields beta near zero (p=0.56). This is the most convincing evidence for causal interpretation.
- **Treatment is not perfectly exogenous**: Swing likely removed TUB mode as a deliberate policy, not randomly. However, the timing was not anticipated by riders, and the cross-city variation in pre-ban TUB adoption provides identification.
- **One post-treatment month**: Only December 2023 is post-treatment. Multi-month post-treatment data (if the ban persisted into 2024) would strengthen the analysis but is not available.

### Dose-Response Scatter
- r = 0.754 (p = 1.67e-10) across 51 cities between TUB share reduction and speeding reduction
- Slope: 0.54 — roughly, each percentage point of TUB share removed eliminates 0.54 percentage points of speeding
