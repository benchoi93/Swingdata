## Key Findings / Observations

### Mode-Switcher Analysis: Composition vs. Compensation (Definitive Evidence)

| Metric | Switchers | Never-TUB | Difference | Cohen's d | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|-----------|----------------|
| Pre-ban STD/ECO speed | 12.94 km/h | 12.30 km/h | +0.65 | 0.237 (small) | Pre-existing gap |
| Dec 2023 STD/ECO speed | 12.85 km/h | 12.01 km/h | +0.85 | 0.290 (small) | Composition effect |
| Within-user speed change | -0.09 km/h | -0.29 km/h | +0.20 | 0.069 (negligible) | No compensation |
| Speeding rate change | -1.71pp | -0.27pp | -1.44pp | -0.170 (negligible) | Switchers' speeding dropped MORE |

**Key points:**
1. Switchers were already 0.65 km/h faster in STD/ECO mode BEFORE the ban -- they are inherently faster riders
2. After the ban, switchers' individual speeds DECREASED slightly (-0.09 km/h), not increased
3. The user-level DiD effect is +0.20 km/h (d=0.069, negligible) -- no evidence of compensation
4. Critically, switchers' speeding rate fell MORE than never-TUB riders (-1.71pp vs -0.27pp)
5. Sample: 37,024 switchers and 79,668 never-TUB users with both pre and post STD/ECO trips
