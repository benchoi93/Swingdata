## Key Findings / Observations

### Kaplan-Meier Results

| Group | N | Events | Median Survival (trips) |
|-------|---|--------|------------------------|
| All riders | 864,357 | 414,509 (48.0%) | 29 |
| TUB users | 345,198 | - | 15 |
| Non-TUB users | 519,159 | - | 81 |
| Age <20 | 162,257 | - | 34 |
| Age 20-24 | 229,276 | - | 29 |
| Age 25-29 | 155,573 | - | 30 |
| Age 40+ | 99,414 | - | 37 |

Log-rank test: chi2 = 91,282, p < 0.001. TUB users reach first speeding 5.4x faster than non-TUB users.

### Cox PH Static Model (200K subsample)

| Covariate | HR | 95% CI | p |
|-----------|-----|---------|---|
| ever_tub | 2.85 | [2.79, 2.91] | <0.001 |
| tub_fraction | 1.66 | [1.63, 1.70] | <0.001 |
| log(n_trips) | 0.81 | [0.81, 0.81] | <0.001 |
| Age <20 | 0.85 | [0.83, 0.87] | <0.001 |
| Age 20-24 | 0.99 | [0.97, 1.00] | 0.13 |
| Age 30-34 | 1.03 | [1.00, 1.06] | 0.02 |
| Age 35-39 | 1.05 | [1.01, 1.08] | 0.01 |
| Age 40+ | 1.06 | [1.03, 1.08] | <0.001 |

C-index = 0.691. Interesting: Age <20 is *protective* for time-to-first-speeding (HR=0.85), despite having higher eventual speeding rates. This likely reflects that young riders take longer to adopt TUB mode.

### Cox Time-Varying Model (100K subsample)

| Covariate | HR | 95% CI | p |
|-----------|-----|---------|---|
| TUB adopted | **10.41** | [10.21, 10.62] | <0.001 |

After a rider adopts TUB mode, their instantaneous hazard of first-speeding increases **10.4x**. This is the within-person causal effect: the same rider, before vs. after TUB adoption.

### TUB Mediation of Experience Effect

| Experience Bin | Overall Speeding | STD/ECO Speeding | TUB Rate |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| 1st trip | 10.3% | 1.2% | 9.5% |
| 2-5 trips | 13.8% | 0.9% | 17.9% |
| 6-20 trips | 19.2% | 0.8% | 28.4% |
| 21-50 trips | 25.0% | 0.8% | 39.1% |
| 51-100 trips | 29.8% | 0.9% | 47.8% |
| 101-500 trips | 34.1% | 1.2% | 56.9% |
| 500+ trips | 33.8% | 1.5% | 58.3% |

**98.4% of the experience-speeding gradient is mediated by TUB adoption.** Within STD/ECO modes, speeding is virtually constant across experience levels (0.8-1.5%). The entire "experience makes riders speed" narrative is actually "experience makes riders choose TUB mode, and TUB enables speeding."
