- TUB mode is the strongest predictor of Habitual Speeder class (RRR=11.8)

- Overall accuracy: 66.3% (Safe Rider: 87.5%, Habitual Speeder: 85.6%, Stop-and-Go: 0%)

### Task 5.7: Robustness Checks

**1. Threshold Sensitivity:**

| Threshold | Speeding Rate | TUB OR | ECO OR | Age 30-34 OR |
|:---------:|:--------:|:------:|:------:|:-----------:|
| 20 km/h | 87.1% | 4.02 | 0.008 | 0.66 |
| **25 km/h** | **30.1%** | **121.2** | **1.15** | **0.69** |
| 30 km/h | 0.2% | 5.14 | 0.53 | 0.42 |

- The extreme TUB OR at 25 km/h is because TUB mode allows max ~25 km/h
- Age effects are stable across thresholds
- ECO mode is protective at 20 and 30 km/h, slightly positive at 25 km/h (selection bias)

## Issues / Blockers
- **Map-matching speed**: LeuvenMapMatching at 5.8s/trip is too slow for 2.83M trips. Need to use osmnx nearest-edge matching (much faster, O(1) per point with spatial index) for road class assignment. This affects Tasks 3.3-3.7 and 5.3-5.4.
- **mappymatch API**: v0.7.1 removed `Geofence.from_bbox()` without backward compatibility. Not worth debugging.
- **Stop-and-Go class prediction**: 0% accuracy from demographics alone. This class is defined by behavioral patterns (frequent stops) that demographics can't explain — this is actually an interesting finding for the paper.
- **ECO mode OR > 1 at 25 km/h threshold**: Confirmed in robustness check. At 20 km/h, ECO OR=0.008 (strongly protective). The anomaly at 25 km/h is because ECO users self-select into TUB mode too, creating confounding.
