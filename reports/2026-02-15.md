## Key Findings / Observations

### Spatial Analysis
- Strong spatial autocorrelation of speeding across all cities (Moran's I 0.16-0.71, all p<0.001)
- Daegu has the strongest clustering (I=0.712) -- likely due to city layout with distinct neighborhoods
- Seoul hot spots concentrate in southern areas (Gangnam/Seocho); cold spots in central/northern Seoul
- Infrastructure overlay reveals hot spots and cold spots have similar frac_major_road (~0.21), but cold spots have 2x more cycling infrastructure (0.033 vs 0.015) -- suggesting cycling infra may reduce speeding

### Two-Part Model (Task 5.4)
- Zero-inflation (70.4%) required hurdle model instead of standard beta regression
- Part 1 (whether any speeding occurs): mode is dominant predictor (TUB OR=126.6); service roads increase probability (OR=1.29); footway decreases it (OR=0.82); unclassified roads strongly protective (OR=0.57)
- Part 2 (speeding intensity given speeding occurs): TUB increases intensity (beta=1.10 on logit scale); cycling infrastructure paradoxically increases intensity when speeding does occur (beta=1.14) -- possibly riders feel safer on bike paths and speed more aggressively
- frac_major_road is protective in both parts (OR=0.62 for occurrence; beta=-0.26 for intensity) -- congestion effect

### Corridor Analysis
- Gangnam: 27 hexes, speeding rates 10-25%, moderate and spatially varied
- Hongdae/Sinchon: 11 hexes, speeding rates 14-26%, generally higher than Gangnam
- Yeouido: 6 hexes, one hex with 80%+ speeding rate (likely riverside cycling path)
